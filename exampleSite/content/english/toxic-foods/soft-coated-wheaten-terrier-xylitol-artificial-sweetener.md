# Is Xylitol (Artificial Sweetener) Toxic to Soft Coated Wheaten Terriers?

**Quick Answer:**
**YES - Xylitol (Artificial Sweetener) is highly toxic to Soft Coated Wheaten Terriers and can be fatal.**

## Symptoms of Xylitol (Artificial Sweetener) Poisoning in Soft Coated Wheaten Terriers

If your Soft Coated Wheaten Terrier has eaten xylitol (artificial sweetener), watch for these symptoms:

Vomiting, loss of coordination, seizures, liver failure, death

**Timeline:** Symptoms typically appear within 
30 minutes to 2 hours
 of ingestion.

---

## What Makes Xylitol (Artificial Sweetener) Toxic?

The toxic compound in xylitol (artificial sweetener) is **Xylitol**.

**How it works:** Xylitol causes a rapid drop in blood sugar (hypoglycemia) and can lead to liver failure. Even small amounts can be dangerous.

## What to Do If Your Soft Coated Wheaten Terrier Ate Xylitol (Artificial Sweetener)

### Immediate Steps:

1. **Remove the source** - Get any remaining xylitol (artificial sweetener) away from your dog
2. **Call your veterinarian** - Even if symptoms seem mild, professional guidance is essential
3. **Do NOT induce vomiting** unless specifically instructed by your veterinarian
4. **Save the packaging** - Bring any packaging or remnants to help identify amount ingested

### When to Call Emergency Vet:

- **Immediately** - Any amount of xylitol (artificial sweetener) requires emergency care

## Treatment for Xylitol (Artificial Sweetener) Poisoning

Treatment varies based on the type of toxin and severity:

**Common treatments may include:**
- Induced vomiting (if caught early and safe to do so)
- Activated charcoal to absorb toxins
- IV fluids to support kidney function
- Medications to control symptoms
- Hospitalization for monitoring

**Recovery time:** Depends on severity and how quickly treatment was started. 
Severe cases may require several days of hospitalization.
