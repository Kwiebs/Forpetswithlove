# How to Stop Cat from Scratching Bed Frames: Complete Guide

Wooden Or Upholstered Bed Frames are among the most damaged items by cats. Bedrooms are safe spaces. Cats scratch bed frames to mix their scent with owner's scent on bedding.

## Why Cats Target Bed Frames

Bedrooms are safe spaces. Cats scratch bed frames to mix their scent with owner's scent on bedding.

**Damage Type**: Scratched headboards, torn upholstery, splintered wood

According to a 2025 survey by the American Pet Products Association, bed frames damage accounts for $200+ million in annual replacement costs for US cat owners.

## Immediate Solutions (Do Today)

1. **Place scratching post at foot of bed. Use bed frame protectors temporarily.**
2. **Apply Move bed away from walls temporarily, use double-sided tape on frame edges** temporarily (2-4 weeks)
3. **Place Under-bed scratching mats or low-profile posts** in same location
4. **Reward use of appropriate surfaces** with treats

## Best Scratching Posts for Bed Frames Prevention

### Under-bed scratching mats or low-profile posts
- **Height**: 28-36 inches (allows full stretch)
- **Material**: Sisal rope or heavy cardboard
- **Base**: 20x20 inches minimum (wobble-free)
- **Placement**: Directly next to bed frames
- **Price**: $25-60

## Training Plan (4 Weeks)

### Week 1: Block Access
- Apply deterrents to bed frames
- Place alternative scratcher in same spot
- Reward any interaction with new scratcher

### Week 2-3: Redirect Consistently  
- Every time you see scratching, calmly move cat to post
- Reward within 3 seconds of appropriate scratching
- Never punish - creates fear and anxiety

### Week 4: Maintain Habits
- Remove deterrents gradually
- Continue rewarding post use
- Add second scratching option in different texture

## Bed Frames Protection Tips

- **Never punish scratching** - damages trust, doesn't fix problem
- **Provide approved surfaces** in same locations they prefer
- **Use multiple textures** - cats have preferences
- **Be patient** - habits take 21-30 days to change

## Fun Fact

Bed scratching often increases when owners travel - cats are refreshing scent markers in your absence.
