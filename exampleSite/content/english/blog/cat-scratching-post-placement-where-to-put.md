# Where to Put a Cat Scratching Post: Placement Guide for 2026

Location matters more than you think. Learn the best places to put scratching posts so your cat actually uses them — based on feline behavior science.

## Understanding the Problem: Scratching Post Placement

Cats scratch in strategic locations to mark territory, stretch after waking, and communicate with other cats. Placement determines whether they use the post or your furniture.

**Surface type:** N/A — placement strategy

**Potential damage:** Post goes unused, continued furniture scratching

## Immediate Solution

Place posts next to where your cat already scratches, near sleeping areas, and in high-traffic rooms.

## Best Scratching Post for This Situation

Multiple posts in strategic locations throughout the home

## Deterrent Strategies

Remove access to previously scratched furniture areas if possible

## Training Tips

Cats scratch to mark territory, so posts need to be in 'important' locations — near entryways, sleeping spots, and social areas. A post in a forgotten corner will be ignored.

## Timeline: Immediate with proper placement

## Cost of Inaction

Wasted money on unused posts, continued furniture damage

## Did You Know?

Cats scratch most within 10 minutes of waking up. Placing a post right next to their favorite sleeping spot dramatically increases usage.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Should I put a scratching post in every room?

At minimum, place posts in rooms where your cat spends the most time and near any furniture they've previously scratched. For multi-cat households, provide one post per cat plus one extra.

### Why does my cat ignore the scratching post I bought?

The most common reason is poor placement. Cats scratch to mark territory, so a post in an unused room won't attract attention. Move it to a high-traffic area or right next to the furniture they've been scratching.

### Can I move a scratching post after my cat starts using it?

Yes, but gradually. Move it a few inches per day toward your desired location. Moving it too quickly may cause your cat to revert to scratching furniture.

### Where should I place a scratching post for multiple cats?

In multi-cat households, place posts in shared social areas and near resources (food, litter boxes). This reduces territorial conflict and gives each cat access to a scratching surface.
