# Guinea Pig Safe Foods List
Guinea pigs cannot produce their own vitamin C, making proper diet crucial for their health.
## Daily Staples
### Hay (Unlimited)
- Timothy hay (adults)
- Alfalfa hay (pregnant or young guinea pigs only)
### Pellets (1/8 cup per day)
Choose plain pellets without added seeds or colored pieces.
## Safe Vegetables (Daily)
- Bell peppers (high in Vitamin C!)
- Romaine lettuce
- Cucumber
- Cilantro
- Parsley
- Carrots (limited due to sugar)
## Occasional Fruits
- Apple (no seeds)
- Strawberry
- Blueberries
- Watermelon
## Foods to AVOID
- Onions and garlic
- Potatoes
- Avocado
- Chocolate
- Dairy products
- Bread and grains
## Signs of Vitamin C Deficiency
Watch for:
- Rough coat
- Lethargy
- Joint swelling
- Slow wound healing
Consider vitamin C supplements if your pig won't eat bell peppers.
