# Seasonal Dog Care Guide
Dogs need different care as seasons change. Here's how to keep them safe year-round.
## Summer Heat Safety
### Never Leave in Hot Cars
Even 70°F outside, car reaches 89°F in 10 minutes. This kills dogs.
### Hot Pavement Burns
If pavement is too hot for your hand (7 seconds test), it's too hot for paws.
### Heat Stroke Signs
- Excessive panting
- Drooling
- Red gums
- Vomiting
- Collapse
- **Cool slowly, see vet immediately**
### Summer Hydration
- Always bring water
- Offer water frequently
- Add water to food
- Use pet water fountains
### Swimming Safety
- Not all dogs can swim
- Rinse chlorine/salt water after
- Watch for ear infections
- Life jackets for poor swimmers
## Winter Cold Care
### Cold Weather Limits
- Under 45°F: Some dogs get cold
- Under 32°F: Most dogs need protection
- Under 20°F: Limit outdoor time
### Winter Hazards
- Antifreeze (sweet but deadly)
- Rock salt on paws
- Hypothermia
- Frostbite on ears and paws
### Winter Gear
- Sweaters for short-haired breeds
- Booties to protect paws
- Reflective gear for visibility
- Warming mats for outdoor dogs
### Winter Grooming
Don't shave double coats—it actually provides insulation. Keep fur trimmed around paws.
### Indoor Air Quality
Heated homes are dry. Consider:
- Humidifier
- Nose and paw balms
- More water intake
