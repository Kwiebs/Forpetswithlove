# Best Scratching Posts for Kittens in 2026 (Expert Picks)

Kittens start scratching early — give them the right post from day one. Our vet-reviewed guide to the best scratching posts for kittens under 12 months.

## Understanding the Problem: Kitten Scratching Needs

Kittens scratch to develop coordination, stretch growing muscles, and establish territory habits. Starting early with the right post prevents bad habits.

**Surface type:** various (sisal, cardboard, carpet)

**Potential damage:** Furniture damage, carpet tearing, curtain climbing

## Immediate Solution

Provide multiple small scratching surfaces at kitten height. Gradually introduce taller posts as they grow.

## Best Scratching Post for This Situation

Low-profile scratcher (12-18 inches) with sisal or cardboard surface

## Deterrent Strategies

Bitter apple spray on furniture corners, double-sided tape

## Training Tips

Kittens learn fast. Place posts near sleeping areas and play zones. Reward with treats immediately after they use the post.

## Timeline: 1-2 weeks for habit formation

## Cost of Inaction

Lifetime of furniture scratching habits

## Did You Know?

Kittens begin scratching at around 8 weeks old — the perfect time to introduce their first scratching post.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### At what age should I get a scratching post for my kitten?

As soon as you bring your kitten home, typically 8-12 weeks. Early introduction makes the post a natural part of their environment and prevents furniture scratching habits.

### What scratching material do kittens prefer?

Most kittens love cardboard and sisal. Offer both types — flat cardboard scratchers and small sisal posts — to discover your kitten's preference.

### How tall should a kitten scratching post be?

Start with 12-18 inch posts that match your kitten's size. Upgrade to 30+ inch posts as they grow to ensure they can fully stretch.

### My kitten ignores the scratching post. What should I do?

Try rubbing catnip on the post, dangling toys near it, or placing it in a high-traffic area. Some kittens prefer horizontal scratchers over vertical posts — experiment with both.
