## Project 1: cardboard box scratcher (beginner - free)

### Why This Works
Cats LOVE cardboard. It's the perfect texture for scratching, and it's completely free if you have boxes.

### Materials Needed
- Cardboard boxes** (medium to large)
- **Box cutter or scissors**
- **Tape or glue**
- **Optional: Catnip

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### Step 1: Prepare Your Boxes
- Flatten cardboard boxes
- Remove tape and staples
- Cut into uniform strips (2-3 inches wide)

#### Step 2: Create the Base
- Stack cardboard layers to create a thick base
- Glue or tape layers together
- Make base at least 18x18 inches for stability

#### Step 3: Build the Scratching Surface
- Roll cardboard strips tightly
- Secure with tape or glue
- Stack rolls to desired height (6-12 inches)

#### Step 4: Final Assembly
- Attach scratching surface to base
- Ensure everything is secure
- Test stability

#### Step 5: Introduce to Your Cat
- Sprinkle catnip on surface
- Place near favorite scratching spot
- Reward with treats when used

**Time Required:** 30-45 minutes  
**Cost:** FREE (if you have boxes)  
**Durability:** 2-4 months  
**Success Rate:** 85%

## Project 2: corrugated cardboard roll (beginner - $5-10)

### Why This Works
Corrugated cardboard provides the perfect scratching texture—dense, durable, and satisfying for cats.

### Materials Needed
- **Corrugated cardboard sheets** (available at office supply stores)
- **Wood base** (18x18 inches)
- **Wood glue**
- **Box cutter**
- **Clamps or heavy books**

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### Step 1: Prepare Cardboard
- Cut cardboard into strips (2-3 inches wide)
- Make strips as long as your desired height
- Cut enough strips to fill your base

#### Step 2: Prepare the Base
- Sand wood base smooth
- Apply wood glue to base
- Position first cardboard strip

#### Step 3: Build the Roll
- Roll cardboard strips tightly
- Glue each layer to the next
- Continue until desired height (6-12 inches)

#### Step 4: Secure and Dry
- Use clamps or heavy books to compress
- Let dry for 24 hours
- Remove clamps

#### Step 5: Final Touches
- Trim any uneven edges
- Sand rough spots
- Add catnip

**Time Required:** 1-2 hours  
**Cost:** $5-10  
**Durability:** 3-6 months  
**Success Rate:** 90%

## Project 3: sisal rope post (intermediate - $15-30)

### Why This Works
Sisal rope is the gold standard for scratching posts—durable, textured, and cats love it.

### Materials Needed
- **Wood post** (4x4 inches, 32-36 inches tall)
- **Wood base** (24x24 inches)
- **Sisal rope** (100+ feet)
- **Wood glue**
- **Screws**
- **Drill**

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### Step 1: Prepare the Base
- Cut wood base to 24x24 inches
- Sand all surfaces smooth
- Mark center for post placement

#### Step 2: Attach the Post
- Drill pilot holes in base
- Secure post with screws
- Ensure post is perfectly vertical

#### Step 3: Start Wrapping
- Apply wood glue to post bottom
- Start wrapping sisal rope tightly
- Overlap each wrap slightly

#### Step 4: Continue Wrapping
- Continue wrapping upward
- Keep tension consistent
- Apply glue as needed

#### Step 5: Secure Top
- Glue and nail rope at top
- Trim excess rope
- Let dry completely

**Time Required:** 2-3 hours  
**Cost:** $15-30  
**Durability:** 6-12 months  
**Success Rate:** 95%

## Project 4: wall-mounted scratcher (intermediate - $10-20)

### Why This Works
Wall-mounted scratchers save floor space and provide vertical scratching options cats love.

### Materials Needed
- **Corrugated cardboard** or **sisal rope**
- **Wood board** (24x36 inches)
- **Mounting hardware** (screws, wall anchors)
- **Wood glue**
- **Level**

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### Step 1: Choose Location
- Select wall near cat's favorite spot
- Ensure wall can support weight
- Mark mounting height (12-24 inches from floor)

#### Step 2: Prepare Board
- Cut board to desired size
- Sand smooth
- Apply glue for scratching surface

#### Step 3: Attach Scratching Surface
- For cardboard: Glue strips tightly
- For sisal: Wrap rope around board
- Let dry completely

#### Step 4: Mount to Wall
- Use level to mark positions
- Drill pilot holes
- Secure with screws and anchors

#### Step 5: Test Stability
- Apply pressure to test
- Ensure no wobbling
- Introduce to cat

**Time Required:** 2-3 hours  
**Cost:** $10-20  
**Durability:** 4-8 months  
**Success Rate:** 88%

## Essential tips for success

### Stability is Non-Negotiable
**Why it matters:** Cats won't use wobbly posts

**How to ensure stability:**
- Use wide, heavy bases
- Add weight (sand, rocks)
- Secure all connections
- Test before introducing to cat

### Material Quality Matters
**Best materials:**
- Cardboard: Corrugated, dense
- Sisal rope: Natural, durable
- Wood: Untreated, smooth
- Glue: Wood glue, non-toxic

**Avoid:**
- Treated wood (chemicals)
- Flimsy cardboard
- Cheap rope (frays quickly)
- Toxic glues

### Placement Strategy
**Where to put your DIY post:**
- Near currently-scratched furniture
- In high-traffic areas
- Near sleeping spots
- Window areas

## Troubleshooting common problems

### Problem: "My cat won't use the DIY post"

**Solutions:**
1. **Add catnip** - Sprinkle generously
2. **Move it closer** - Within 3 feet of scratched furniture
3. **Try different texture** - Cardboard vs sisal
4. **Check stability** - Ensure no wobbling
5. **Use positive reinforcement** - Treats and praise

### Problem: "The post falls apart"

**Solutions:**
1. **Use more glue** - Don't skimp on adhesive
2. **Add screws** - Reinforce with hardware
3. **Heavier base** - Add weight
4. **Better materials** - Use quality cardboard/rope
5. **Let dry completely** - Don't rush

### Problem: "It looks ugly"

**Solutions:**
1. **Paint or stain** - Match your decor
2. **Cover with fabric** - Hide cardboard
3. **Use attractive wood** - Natural finish
4. **Add decorations** - Cat-themed accents
5. **Embrace the rustic look** - DIY charm
