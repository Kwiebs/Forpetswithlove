## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the best rope for a DIY scratching post?

Sisal rope (6mm or 8mm) is the gold standard. It's durable, provides excellent claw grip, and is safe if ingested in small amounts. Avoid synthetic ropes that can fray into dangerous strands.

### How tall should a DIY scratching post be?

Minimum 30 inches for adult cats, 34-36 inches for large breeds. The key is that your cat should be able to fully stretch without reaching the top.

### How do I make sure the post doesn't tip over?

Use a base that's at least 16x16 inches made from 3/4-inch plywood. Add sandbags or extra weight inside the base. The general rule: the base width should be at least 1/3 of the post height.

### Can I use carpet instead of sisal rope?

Carpet works but wears out faster than sisal and may encourage your cat to scratch other carpeted surfaces. If using carpet, choose a different texture from your home's carpet to avoid confusion.
