# New Puppy Checklist: Everything You Need
Don't wait until bring-home day to realize you're missing essential supplies.
## Essential Supplies
### Sleeping
- [ ] Dog bed (washable, appropriately sized)
- [ ] Crate with divider (for growing puppies)
- [ ] Old towels for crate comfort
- [ ] Radio or white noise machine
### Food & Water
- [ ] Quality puppy food (research brands first)
- [ ] Food bowls (stainless steel, easy to clean)
- [ ] Water bowl (or pet fountain)
- [ ] Storage container for food
### Training
- [ ] Training treats (small, low-calorie)
- [ ] Clicker for clicker training
- [ ] Puppy pads for housetraining
- [ ] Enzymatic cleaner for accidents
- [ ] Long leash (6-10 feet) for training
- [ ] Short leash for walks
### Grooming
- [ ] Puppy brush or comb
- [ ] Puppy shampoo (gentle formula)
- [ ] Nail clippers or grinder
- [ ] Toothbrush and puppy toothpaste
- [ ] Ear cleaner
### Safety
- [ ] Collar with ID tag
- [ ] Microchip registration
- [ ] Baby gates to block rooms
- [ ] Cable cord covers
- [ ] Trash can with lid
- [ ] Emergency vet contact info
## Before You Bring Puppy Home
- [ ] Schedule vet appointment within 48 hours
- [ ] Puppy-proof your home
- [ ] Establish bathroom area
- [ ] Inform landlord (if renting)
- [ ] Stock up on your own food—puppies are hungry work!
## First Week Priorities
1. Bond with puppy
2. Establish routine
3. Begin housetraining
4. Introduce to family members
5. Schedule vet checkup
