# How to Teach Your Dog to Fetch

Fetch is more than just exercise—it's a bonding activity that engages your dog's natural instincts.

## Why Fetch Matters

- Burns excess energy
- Stimulates mind
- Strengthens bond with owner
- Provides structured playtime

## Step-by-Step Training

### Phase 1: The Pick-Up
Start by rewarding any interest in the toy. Drop it near your dog and praise when they investigate.

### Phase 2: The Grab
Hold the toy and say "take it." Reward grabbing behavior immediately.

### Phase 3: The Retrieve
Toss the toy just a few feet. When your dog picks it up, say "fetch!" and encourage them to return.

### Phase 4: The Release
Trade the toy for a treat. Say "drop it" and reward the release.

## Fetch Variations

1. **Tug-Fetch**: Combine fetch with gentle tug games
2. **Hide and Seek**: Hide the toy for added mental stimulation
3. **Multiple Toys**: Teach them to fetch different objects by name
