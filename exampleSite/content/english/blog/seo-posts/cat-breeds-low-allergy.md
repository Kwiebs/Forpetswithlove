# Best Cat Breeds for Allergy Sufferers

While no cat is truly 100% hypoallergenic, some breeds produce fewer allergens than others.

## Understanding Cat Allergies

Cat allergies are usually triggered by:
- **Fel d 1 protein** in saliva and skin glands
- Proteins in cat urine
- Dander (dead skin cells)

When cats groom, they spread allergens onto their fur.

## Best Breeds for Allergies

### 1. Siberian
- Produces lower levels of Fel d 1
- Beautiful long coat
- Playful, dog-like personality

### 2. Balinese
- Less fur means fewer allergens
- Elegant, medium-length coat
- Vocal and affectionate

### 3. Russian Blue
- Dense double coat traps allergens
- Quiet and gentle
- Low grooming needs

### 4. Devon Rex
- Minimal shedding
- Curly, short coat
- Dog-like loyalty

### 5. Cornish Rex
- Only has undercoat
- Wavy fur that doesn't shed much
- Playful throughout life

## Tips for Living with Cats and Allergies

1. Brush cat daily (someone without allergies should brush)
2. Use HEPA air purifiers
3. Keep bedroom cat-free zone
4. Wash bedding weekly
5. Consider immunotherapy shots

## Disclaimer

Always spend time with a cat before adopting if you have allergies. Individual reactions vary greatly.
