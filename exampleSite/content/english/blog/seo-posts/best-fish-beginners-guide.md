# Best Fish for Beginners

The right first fish makes all the difference. Here are the most forgiving species.

## Tank Setup First

Don't buy fish before your tank is ready:

1. Set up tank and equipment
2. Run nitrogen cycle (4-6 weeks)
3. Test water parameters daily
4. Add fish slowly once cycled

## Top Beginner Fish

### 1. Betta Fish
**Tank Size:** 5 gallons minimum
**Temperature:** 76-82°F
**Why They're Great:** Beautiful colors, personality, can live alone

### 2. Neon Tetras
**Tank Size:** 10 gallons minimum
**Temperature:** 70-77°F
**Why They're Great:** Colorful, peaceful, schooling fish (keep in groups of 6+)

### 3. Guppies
**Tank Size:** 5 gallons minimum
**Temperature:** 72-82°F
**Why They're Great:** Colorful, breed readily, very hardy

### 4. Platies
**Tank Size:** 10 gallons
**Temperature:** 70-77°F
**Why They're Great:** Peaceful, many colors, easy to breed

### 5. Corydoras Catfish
**Tank Size:** 10 gallons
**Temperature:** 72-78°F
**Why They're Great:** Bottom feeder keeps tank clean, peaceful, schooling

### 6. Mollies
**Tank Size:** 20 gallons
**Temperature:** 72-78°F
**Why They're Great:** Hardy, various colors, livebearers

### 7. Goldfish (Single Tail)
**Tank Size:** 20 gallons per fish
**Temperature:** 65-72°F (cold water)
**Why They're Great:** Classic pet, long-lived

## Fish to Avoid as Beginners

❌ Angelfish (needs large tank)
❌ Plecos (grow huge)
❌ Cichlids (aggressive)
❌ Flowerhorn (aggressive, large)
❌ Common Pleco (grows to 2 feet!)

## Beginner Tank Mates

Compatible combinations:
- Betta + Corydoras
- Neon Tetras + Guppies
- Platies + Mollies + Corydoras

## Essential Equipment

- Filter (crucial!)
- Heater (for tropical fish)
- Thermometer
- Water test kit
- Gravel vacuum
- Water conditioner
