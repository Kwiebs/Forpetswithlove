# Best Dog Breeds for Apartment Living

Big dogs can live in small spaces, and small dogs can be terrible apartment pets. Here's what actually matters.

## What Makes a Good Apartment Dog?

### Energy Level
Low to moderate energy dogs adapt best. They should be content with 2-3 walks daily plus indoor play.

### Barking Level
Some small dogs bark excessively. Look for breeds known to be quiet.

### Size
Smaller is generally easier, but a large calm dog can thrive in an apartment.

### Socialization Needs
Some dogs experience separation anxiety in small spaces. Choose accordingly.

## Top Apartment Breeds

### French Bulldog
- Low exercise needs
- Minimal barking
- Compact size
- Great with apartments

### Cavalier King Charles Spaniel
- Adaptable and calm
- Medium size
- Sweet temperament

### Greyhound
- Surprising apartment dogs!
- 45-80 lbs but calm indoors
- Need daily walks but are couch potatoes

### Basset Hound
- Low energy
- Docile temperament
- Medium size
- Moderate barking

### Shih Tzu
- Small and adaptable
- Affectionate
- Low exercise needs

## Breeds to Avoid

- Huskies (high energy, need space)
- Border Collies (mental stimulation needs)
- Dalmatians (high energy)
- Most terriers (barking tendency)

## Apartment Dog Tips

1. Walk at least twice daily
2. Mental stimulation through puzzle toys
3. Train early to reduce barking
4. Consider dog daycare for socialization
5. Vertical space matters—tall furniture isn't an obstacle
