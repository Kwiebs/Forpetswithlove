## Breed-Specific Buying Guide

### Height Requirements
Maine Coon cats need posts **36+ inches** tall. This allows full vertical stretching, which is critical for their vertical, aggressive climbers style.

### Base Stability
With 12-18 lbs weight, the base must be **24x24 inches minimum** minimum. Anything smaller will tip when they lean full weight against it.

### Material Selection
Choose **sisal rope (durable)** based on their very strong claws. This breed's scratching style (vertical, aggressive climbers) works best with this texture.

## Common Maine Coon Scratching Problems

### Problem: Standard posts tip over when they lean full weight
**Solution**: Extra stability due to size and climbing behavior. Needs tall posts they can fully stretch on. Provide alternatives that match their natural scratching preferences.

### Problem: Post Tips Over
**Solution**: Upgrade to 24x24 inches minimum base. Maine Coon cats at 12-18 lbs need commercial-grade stability.

### Problem: Ignoring the Post
**Solution**: Place near their favorite scratching spot. Use catnip or silvervine. Maine Coons are one of the largest domesticated cat breeds and need proportional scratching equipment.

## DIY Maine Coon Scratching Post

Create a breed-appropriate post:
1. Cut plywood base to 24x24 inches minimum
2. Attach 4x4 post cut to 36+ inches
3. Wrap with sisal rope (durable)
4. Secure with heavy-duty staples
5. Add catnip and place in favorite scratching area
